## 🎯 Risk Assessment

### Overall Risk: **HIGH**
- **Risk Score**: 0.85/1.0
- **Primary Concerns**:
  1. Developmental neurotoxicity
  2. Renal toxicity
  3. Systemic lead exposure
  4. Environmental persistence

### Mitigation Strategies Recommended:
1. **Material Encapsulation**: Prevent degradation and lead release
2. **Lead-Free Alternatives**: Use tin-based perovskites instead
3. **Proper Disposal**: Implement recycling protocols for perovskite materials
4. **Occupational Safety**: Enforce safety measures during manufacturing
5. **Environmental Monitoring**: Track lead release in production and use
6. **Stable Formulations**: Develop non-toxic perovskite formulations

## 🔍 Scientific Findings

### Key Mechanisms Identified:
1. **Lead Ion Release**: Primary molecular initiating event
2. **Bioavailability**: Gastrointestinal absorption and blood-brain barrier penetration
3. **Toxicokinetics**: Distribution to target organs (brain, kidneys, developing tissues)
4. **Molecular Targets**: Calcium-dependent processes, mitochondrial function, neurotransmitter systems
5. **Cellular Effects**: Oxidative stress, apoptosis, DNA damage
6. **Systemic Outcomes**: Organ-specific toxicity and developmental abnormalities

### Evidence Strength:
- **Strong Evidence**: Neurotoxicity, renal toxicity, developmental toxicity pathways
- **Moderate Evidence**: Degradation products toxicity, immune system modulation
- **Consistent with Literature**: All pathways align with established lead toxicity mechanisms

## 📝 Conclusion

This project successfully constructed 5 comprehensive Adverse Outcome Pathways for methylammonium lead iodide perovskites, providing a robust scientific foundation for understanding and mitigating the toxicological risks associated with these materials. The constructed AOPs demonstrate clear pathways from molecular initiating events to adverse health outcomes, with strong evidence supporting the neurotoxic, renal toxic, and developmental toxic effects of lead released from perovskite degradation.

The high overall risk score (0.85) and identification of multiple adverse outcomes underscore the need for careful handling, proper encapsulation, and consideration of lead-free alternatives in perovskite material applications. This work serves as both a scientific contribution to the field of nanotoxicology and a practical resource for industry, regulators, and researchers working with perovskite materials.
